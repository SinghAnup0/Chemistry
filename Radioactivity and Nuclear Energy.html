Q5: Who invented radioactivity?

Source: RRB NTPC 16.04.2016 (Shift-III) Stage Ist
Correct! Some invisible rays are automatically released from certain elements and their compounds. These rays are called radioactive rays or Becquerel rays. The phenomenon of rays emitting automatically from a substance is called radioactivity. It was first discovered by the French scientist Henri Becquerel. The major radioactive elements are uranium, thorium, polonium, plutonium etc. Pierre Curie and Madame Curie later discovered the most important radioactive element, radium.
Incorrect. Some invisible rays are automatically released from certain elements and their compounds. These rays are called radioactive rays or Becquerel rays. The phenomenon of rays emitting automatically from a substance is called radioactivity. It was first discovered by the French scientist Henri Becquerel. The major radioactive elements are uranium, thorium, polonium, plutonium etc. Pierre Curie and Madame Curie later discovered the most important radioactive element, radium.
