Q7: Dalton's law of partial pressure applies to which of the following equations?

Source: RRB Group-D 02-11-2018 (Shift-III)
Correct! If two or more gases are kept in a closed vessel at certain temperature and certain pressure, but there is no chemical reaction, then their total pressure in the vessel is equal to the sum of the partial pressure of different gases. This is called Dalton's law of partial pressure. Suppose the total pressure (z) of a gaseous mixture and the pressure of the gases present are p1, p2, p3 ...... pn, then according to Dalton's partial pressure rules - P = p1 + p2 + p3 + ........ + pn.
